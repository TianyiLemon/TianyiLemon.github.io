Cross-Characteristic Gate Complexity: Upper Bounds and Structural Theorems

Yipin Wang

Preprint, 2026

We study the minimum number of "gates" — compositions of affine maps $\mathbb{F}_q^n \to \mathbb{F}_q$ with arbitrary functions $\mathbb{F}_q \to \mathbb{F}_p$ — needed to represent the indicator function of the algebraic torus $(\mathbb{F}_q^*)^n \subset \mathbb{F}_q^n$, where $p$ and $q$ are distinct primes. We formulate gate complexity as a minimum coset weight problem in a cross-characteristic linear code, prove $t(p, 2, n) = 2n - 1$ for all primes $p \geq 3$ via Walsh–Fourier analysis, and establish $t(2, 3, n) = 2^{n-1}$ for all $n$ via a coordinate induction on $\mathbb{F}_4$-Fourier support exploiting the Vandermonde structure of the DFT matrix.

Spanning Trees, Modular Symbols, and a New Arithmetic Invariant of Elliptic Curves

Yipin Wang

Research notes, 2026

We observe that averaging the plus modular symbol $\{0, t/u\}^+$ over the feasible vectors of Alon–Bucić–Gishboliner (spanning tree counts of series-parallel graphs) gives a rational limit $c_f \in \mathbb{Q}$ for each weight-2 newform $f$. The proof reduces to a Markov chain on $\mathbb{P}^1(\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})$ with geometric transition weights. The product $\lambda_f = c_f \cdot \Omega^+$ is an isogeny invariant, computed for all 93 optimal curves of conductor $\leq 100$. We find that the denominators of $c_f$ are governed by a single determinant depending only on $N$, and that "alien primes" in these denominators arise as norms in the splitting field of an irreducible Steinberg characteristic polynomial. All computed values satisfy $c_f > 0$, but we do not know how to prove this in general.

On the 2-adic Structure of Zagier's MZV Matrices

Yipin Wang

Submitted to Proceedings of the AMS, 2026

We investigate the 2-adic properties of the inverse of Zagier's matrix $M_K$, which expresses Hoffman elements $H(a, b) = \zeta(\underbrace{2,\ldots,2}_a, 3, \underbrace{2,\ldots,2}_b)$ as rational linear combinations of products $\zeta(2)^m \zeta(2n + 1)$. We prove that all entries in the last row of $(M_K)^{-1}$ have 2-adic valuation zero (Theorem 2.1), implying that all coefficients in the decomposition of $\zeta(2)^{K-1}\zeta(3)$ into the Hoffman basis are odd integers. More generally, we establish a row minimum formula (Theorem 2.5): the minimum 2-adic valuation in row $K-1-i$ of $(M_K)^{-1}$ equals $2i - v_2(i+1)$. As a companion result, we give a closed-form inverse for the binomial core matrix $B_N[a,i] = \binom{2i}{2a}$ in terms of Euler–secant numbers, with the exact 2-adic valuation governed by binary carry counting via Kummer's theorem.

A Fourier-Analytic Switching Lemma over $\mathbb{F}_p$ and the AC$^0$ Lower Bound for Generalized Parity

Yipin Wang

Preprint, 2026

We prove a switching lemma for constant-depth circuits over the alphabet $\mathbb{F}_p$ with generalized AND/OR gates, extending Tal's Fourier-analytic approach from the Boolean setting. The key new ingredient is a direct computation of the $L_1$ Fourier mass of AND/OR gates over $\mathbb{F}_p$, which yields the lower bound $L_1^{\geq s}(\mathrm{AND}_s) = ((p-1)/p)^s$ without any conjectural input. As a consequence, we obtain that for any prime $p$, constant-depth circuits of sub-exponential size over $\mathbb{F}_p$ cannot compute $\mathbf{1}[\sum_i x_i \equiv 0 \pmod{p}]$.
